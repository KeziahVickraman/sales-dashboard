User Manual — how to use this dashboard

Building your dashboard from scratch

Click Upload Excel in the top-right corner and select your sales_template.xlsx file. The dashboard will read the full_data sheet (and attrition_analysis if present) and instantly populate all tabs — Overview, Portfolio, Historical Trend, Attrition Prediction, and Revenue Forecast — with your live data. The badge in the top-left will switch from Demo data to Live data to confirm.

Sharing your dashboard with a colleague

Once your data is loaded, click Export JSON in the top-right corner. This downloads a snapshot file (e.g. dashboard-2026-05-26.json) containing all your employee data, attrition figures, and any scenario settings you have edited. Send this file to your colleague — via email, Teams, or any file transfer.

Viewing a colleague's dashboard

Received a JSON snapshot from a colleague? Click Import JSON in the top-right corner and select the file. The dashboard will load exactly as they left it — same data, same scenario growth rates — so you can explore and analyse without needing the original Excel file.

1. Historical baseline

2025 is the live portfolio total from the loaded data. 2023 and 2024 are estimated from the 2025 base while real historical sheets are not yet wired in:

2024 revenue ≈ 2025 revenue × 0.82
2023 revenue ≈ 2025 revenue × 0.68

Where do 0.82 and 0.68 come from? They are not derived from your data — they are hardcoded round-number placeholders. Reverse-engineering what they imply:

  • 1 ÷ 0.82 ≈ 1.22 → an implied ~22% YoY growth from 2024 to 2025
  • 0.82 ÷ 0.68 ≈ 1.21 → another ~21% YoY growth from 2023 to 2024
  • (1 ÷ 0.68)^(1/2) − 1 ≈ 21% → historical CAGR baked into the chart

So these multipliers encode a working assumption that the portfolio grew roughly 21–22% per year historically — slightly above the Base case's 18% forward growth. They were chosen as plausible round numbers, not fitted to any data.

When the trend_analysis sheet parser is added, these will be replaced with actual reported numbers.

2. Forward projection formula

Revenue compounds year-on-year using scenario-specific growth rates that taper as the base grows:

Year 1 = 2025 × (1 + g₁)
Year 2 = Year 1 × (1 + g₂)
Year n = Year n-1 × (1 + gₙ)

Headcount projection uses the same compounding pattern with separate hcGrowth rates per scenario, then revenue-per-resource is derived as revenue ÷ headcount for each year.

3. Projection CAGR

CAGR = ( (final projected revenue ÷ 2025 revenue) ^ (1 ÷ projected years) − 1 ) × 100%

4. Confidence band

The shaded band around the projected line widens further out, reflecting growing uncertainty:

upper bound = projection × (1 + 0.08 + i × 0.02)
lower bound = projection × (1 − 0.06 − i × 0.015)

Where i is the projection step, starting at 0 for the first projected year. This is illustrative — not a statistical prediction interval.

5. Scenario rationale & rates

The editable table above is the live source of truth for forecast years and scenario growth rates. Added years reuse the final existing growth assumptions until edited.

Base case — assumes the current client pipeline holds, modest rate uplift on renewals, and normal attrition replaced in line with growth. Growth tapers from 18% to 12% as the larger revenue base makes the same dollar increment a smaller percentage.

Bull case — assumes new client wins beyond the current pipeline, above-market rate increases on renewals (driven by skill scarcity), sub-10% attrition, and headcount expansion ahead of revenue to capture demand.

Bear case — assumes pipeline weakness, downward rate pressure from client negotiations or vendor consolidation, elevated attrition with slow replacement, and a hiring freeze. Treat this as a conservative floor, not a worst case.

6. Honest caveats

  • These growth rates are fixed assumptions in the code, not outputs of a fitted model.
  • The "ML Forecast" label reflects the intended direction of this panel, not the current implementation — there is no regression, time-series, or learning component yet.
  • To replace this with a real model: fit on historical revenue from trend_analysis, optionally include macro drivers (client headcount, billable rate index), and export coefficients for in-browser inference.

Current prediction model

The current attrition prediction is a transparent rule-based scoring model, not a trained ML model yet. It uses tenure pattern and gross margin as the strongest signals, then caps the final score at 95.

risk score = 38 + tenure score + GM score

Tenure score

  • < 6 months = +30
  • 6-11 months = +20
  • > 42 months = +18
  • all other tenure bands = +8

Gross margin score

  • GM < 12% = +18
  • GM > 18% = +5
  • all other GM bands = +10

The feature-importance chart reflects this current proxy model. A trained model can replace it once labelled historical attrition outcomes are available.

Health rating

Each placement is assigned a health rating based entirely on its gross margin percentage (GM%). GM% measures how much of the billed revenue is retained as profit after paying the employee's cost-to-company.

GM% = (Annual revenue − Annual CTC) ÷ Annual revenue × 100

Rating thresholds

RatingGM% rangeWhat it means
High marginGM% ≥ 18%Strong profitability — above target. Placement is performing well commercially.
Healthy13% ≤ GM% < 18%Acceptable margin — within the normal range. No immediate commercial concern.
WatchGM% < 13%Below the healthy threshold. May indicate rate pressure, high cost, or a contract requiring renegotiation.

The 13% and 18% thresholds are fixed reference points in the current model. They are not dynamically derived from your portfolio — they represent business targets set for this dashboard. A placement rated Watch is not necessarily unprofitable, but it warrants a closer look at the billing rate or cost structure.
